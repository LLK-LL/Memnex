# Quality Check

## Purpose

Use this reference before returning a completed report section, thesis passage, abstract, or slide outline.

## Logic Check

- Does the background lead naturally to the research problem?
- Is the gap specific enough to justify the proposed work?
- Are goals, research contents, and methods separated clearly?
- Does each section support the thesis title?
- Are completed work and future work distinguishable?

## Evidence Check

- Are datasets, metrics, baselines, modules, figures, and tables named when available?
- Are results marked as preliminary when appropriate?
- Are claims proportional to the current evidence?
- Are unsupported words such as "显著", "最优", "首次", and "突破" removed or justified?

## Chinese Style Check

- Remove empty openers and decorative transitions.
- Replace abstract evaluation with concrete technical meaning.
- Keep technical terms consistent.
- Avoid overlong translated sentences.
- Avoid repeated paragraph endings.

## Thesis/Report Compliance

- Follow the user's school template if provided.
- Preserve required headings.
- Keep citation placeholders or BibTeX keys intact.
- Do not invent references.
- Keep figure/table numbering consistent with the source.
- For LaTeX, preserve commands, labels, citations, equations, and cross-references.

## Defense Readiness

For midterm defense content, verify that the text can answer:

- What problem is being studied?
- Why is it worth studying?
- What has already been completed?
- What evidence supports the current progress?
- What remains unfinished?
- What is the concrete plan before final defense?

## Output Format

When returning edited writing, include:

1. revised text
2. short note on major changes
3. unresolved placeholders or missing evidence

For a review-only request, lead with issues and give actionable revisions.
